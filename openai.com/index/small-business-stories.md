## Fixing equipment

My oven is showing a warning message: ‘AF23.09 Gas Unit Lock.’ What does that mean and what should I try first?

Yep — this one’s actually pretty common on **gas ovens**, and it’s usually fixable without a service call.

**What the warning means**

* **AF23.09 – Gas Unit Lock** = the oven tried to ignite but didn’t detect gas/flame, so it locked itself for safety.

**What you should try (in order):**

1. **Tap “Gas Restart.”** Sometimes it’s just a failed ignition on the first try.
2. **Check the gas supply.** Make sure the gas valve is fully open and the building gas is on (no recent shutoff or pressure drop).
3. **Power-cycle the oven.** Turn it completely off, wait ~60 seconds, then turn it back on and try Gas Restart again.
4. **Let it sit if it keeps locking.** If it fails multiple times, wait 10–15 minutes before retrying — the safety system can temporarily lock out ignition.
5. **Look for obvious airflow issues.** Make sure vents aren’t blocked and the door seals properly.

## Knowing your materials

What happens to 7018 rod if it isn’t kept in an oven? I’ve been told by experienced welders that E71T-11 wire can perform better in dry climates like Nevada. Can you explain?

E7018 is a low-hydrogen rod, which means it’s sensitive to moisture. When it’s not kept in an oven, the flux absorbs water from the air, increasing hydrogen in the weld. That can lead to porosity, weaker welds, and delayed cracking—especially in structural or thick steel applications.
