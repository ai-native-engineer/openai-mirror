# OpenAI for Nonprofits

Updated: 3 days ago

## What is OpenAI for Nonprofits?

OpenAI for Nonprofits is an initiative to enhance the accessibility of our tools for nonprofit organizations. Through OpenAI for Nonprofits, nonprofits can now access ChatGPT Business for $8 per month per user (purchased annually) and $10 per month per user (purchased monthly). Larger nonprofits that are ready for large-scale deployment can [contact our sales team](https://chatgpt.com/contact-sales) to access up to a 75% discount on ChatGPT Enterprise.  
  
---

## Is ChatGPT Business or ChatGPT Enterprise the right fit for my organization?

[ChatGPT Business](/en/articles/8792828-what-is-chatgpt-team) is the best fit for small to medium-sized teams looking for advanced capabilities, collaboration features, centralized billing, and enterprise-grade security features.  
  
  
[ChatGPT Enterprise](https://help.openai.com/en/collections/5688074-chatgpt-enterprise) is the best fit for larger organizations needing API access, customization, and higher level security features.

## How do I apply for a nonprofit discount on ChatGPT Business?

If you are currently a subscriber to ChatGPT Business:

* Fill out this [short form](https://validate.poweredbypercent.com/openai) using the email address associated with your ChatGPT Business account.
* If approved, a prorated discount will apply to your current billing cycle, and the discount will apply to future billing.

If you are not yet a subscriber to ChatGPT Business:

* Fill out this [short form](https://validate.poweredbypercent.com/openai) using the email address you’d like to associate with your ChatGPT Business account.

Once approved, [create an OpenAI account](https://platform.openai.com/signup), and then [sign up for ChatGPT Business](/en/articles/8980713-how-do-i-sign-up-for-chatgpt-team). The nonprofit discount will automatically apply at checkout.

## Do you offer Business Associate Agreements (BAA) for nonprofits?

We do not offer BAAs for ChatGPT Business. If you require a BAA, please [contact our sales team](https://openai.com/contact-sales/) about [ChatGPT Enterprise](https://openai.com/index/introducing-chatgpt-enterprise/).

## Does OpenAI train on the data from ChatGPT Business or ChatGPT Enterprise?

With ChatGPT Business and ChatGPT Enterprise, you own and control your data—we do not train on your data or conversations, and our models don’t learn from your usage. More details on our data privacy practices can be found on our [privacy page](https://openai.com/enterprise-privacy/) and [security portal](https://trust.openai.com/).

## In what ways can ChatGPT assist nonprofits?

ChatGPT can enable nonprofits to achieve greater impact with fewer resources by expediting a wide range of tasks. OpenAI has a number of resources available for helping any user get started with ChatGPT, including: [a guide to prompt engineering](https://platform.openai.com/docs/guides/prompt-engineering/prompt-engineering), [a guide to building GPTs](/en/articles/8770868-gpt-builder), [a guide to extracting insights with ChatGPT Data Analysis](/en/articles/9213685-extracting-insights-with-chatgpt-data-analysis), and [a guide to creating custom instructions](/en/articles/8096356-custom-instructions-for-chatgpt).  
  
If you’re just getting started, it can be helpful to consider specific use cases. We’re sharing a few common nonprofit use cases along with tips below.  
  
---

## Refining grant proposals

Upload a draft proposal and the donor evaluation criteria. Ask ChatGPT to assess your proposal against the criteria and suggest specific opportunities for refinement.  
  
  
*Tip:* Apply **“step-by-step instructions”** to prescribe a specific reasoning process that leads to the final recommendation. For example, you might say:

1. Review the uploaded draft proposal and donor criteria.
2. Evaluate the draft proposal against each donor criterion, providing a score and an explanation for each score.
3. Offer specific feedback on how to improve the proposal to better meet each criterion.
4. Provide an example of an improved narrative based on your feedback.

---

## Sharing research findings

Upload a research paper and ask ChatGPT to explain key takeaways to colleagues who are not familiar with the topic.  
  
  
*Tip:* Ask ChatGPT to **adopt a persona** to help communicate research findings to your colleagues. For example: “You are a research assistant at a nonprofit improving agricultural outcomes for smallholder farmers. Your job is to extract learnings for the latest research that may be helpful for the programs team when designing projects. Summarize this research paper, highlighting key findings that might be relevant to program design.” If you’d like this persona to be a continual resource for your colleagues, you might consider [building a GPT.](https://help.openai.com/en/collections/8475422-building-gpts)  
  
---

## Conduct a sentiment analysis on a social media post

Upload all the comments to a social media post and ask ChatGPT to conduct a sentiment analysis.  
  
  
*Tip:* Give ChatGPT examples of how you’d like it to respond (this method is called “few shot prompting”). For example, you could ask ChatGPT to respond in a chart with columns describing the comment’s topic, mood, and author.
