# Flexible pricing for the Enterprise, Edu, and Business plans

Frequently asked questions and key information about flexible pricing

Updated: 1 hour ago

***Note:*** *Starting June 24, 2026, Codex seats will no longer be available to new ChatGPT Business plans, or to Business workspaces that never added a Codex seat prior to June 24 2026. Business workspaces that had added a Codex seat prior to June 24, 2026, including pending invites as of June 24 can continue to manage and add usage-based Codex seats.*

## How do credits work in ChatGPT plans?

Credits unlock additional, flexible access to advanced ChatGPT features like Deep Research, Thinking models, Image Gen, Advanced Voice and Codex. These features are available across ChatGPT Business, Enterprise, and Edu plans, with varying usage models:

* **Business users** receive per-seat limits for all advanced features. If a user exceeds their limit and the workspace has purchased credits, they can continue drawing from the shared pool. Purchasing credit packs is optional and only necessary if a workspace owner wants to unblock users who exceed their usage limits, or the workspace features [Codex seats](/en/articles/8792828#codex-seats-usage-based-no-fixed-cost).
* **Enterprise and Edu workspaces** purchase a shared credit pool at the contract level. All users and seat types in the workspace draw from this pool when using advanced features. There are no per-seat usage caps by default - owners/admins can use [RBAC](/en/articles/11750701-rbac) to set spend controls by group.

Your current plan hasn't changed, and still includes access to advanced models and capabilities, as well as virtually unlimited access to Instant models, and access to business features like apps, etc.  
  
Unlimited or virtually unlimited access is subject to abuse guardrails and any model-specific usage allowances. Temporary restrictions may apply to prevent misuse.  
  
Review the [ChatGPT rate card](/en/articles/11481834-chatgpt-rate-card-business-enterpriseedu) and the [Codex rate card](/en/articles/20001106-codex-rate-card) for rate information.

## Are credits refundable?

Credits are not refundable, except where required by law or in cases of confirmed account compromise (see below).

## What happens when I reach my credit or usage limit?

* **Business:** Users see a banner when their included usage is exhausted. If no credits are available in the workspace pool, the feature is blocked and users can send an in-product request to their admin to add more. Admins and owners can use [automatic recharge](/en/articles/20001155#use-automatic-recharge) to make sure the workspace has sufficient credits.
* **Enterprise and Edu:** Once the shared credit pool is exhausted, advanced features will be paused unless Workspace Owners enable overages or purchases additional credits through their OpenAI Account team. Workspace Owners receive email and in-product alerts when thresholds are hit.

## How do I buy credits?

* **Business:** Workspace Owners purchase credits via **Settings** > [**Billing**](https://chatgpt.com/admin/billing). Credits are pooled across all users. [Read more](/en/articles/8792536).
* **Enterprise and Edu:** Credits are purchased through your OpenAI Account team as part of your contract. Speak to your rep for details.

## How can I check my credit balance and usage?

Workspace Owners across Business, Enterprise, and Edu plan types can view their remaining credits and download usage reports by going to **Settings** > [**Billing**](https://chatgpt.com/admin/billing).

## Do credits expire or rollover?

* **Business:** Credits are valid for 12 months after purchase.
* **Enterprise and Edu:** Credit allocation and expiration are defined in your Order Form. If you're unsure of your terms, contact your account team.

## Can I use credits for any ChatGPT feature?

Credits only apply to advanced models and features. You do not need credits to use core ChatGPT models and features such as search, file upload, and canvas.

## Can I configure alerts or limits for credit usage?

* **Business:** Workspace Owners can configure credit usage alerts in **Workspace settings** > [**Billing**](https://chatgpt.com/admin/billing) > **Usage Alerts**.
* **Enterprise and Edu:** Workspace Owners can configure usage alerts and hard overage limits in **Workspace settings** > [**Billing**](https://chatgpt.com/admin/billing). **Overage limits** let you set a strict limit on how much their organization can extend usage beyond their committed plan (eg. if overage limits are set to 0, an organization will not be allowed to go into overages).

## What happens if credits are used due to a compromised account?

If an account in your workspace is compromised and credits are spent without authorization, contact [support@openai.com](mailto:support@openai.com) immediately. We will investigate the incident, restore lost credits if misuse is confirmed, and assist with password resets and security controls. We also recommend that you take immediate steps to secure the account by following the steps outlined [here.](/en/articles/7242626-how-can-i-report-fraud-or-suspicious-activity)

## Does ChatGPT charge credits when it auto-routes to a mini model?

No. If ChatGPT automatically routes your message to a **mini** version of a model, **0 credits are used**.

ChatGPT chooses the smallest model that can reliably handle your request. For lightweight tasks (like short questions, quick summaries, or simple rewrites), it may use a mini model behind the scenes. When that happens, your experience stays the same - but no credits are deducted. Only messages that run on full-size models use credits.
