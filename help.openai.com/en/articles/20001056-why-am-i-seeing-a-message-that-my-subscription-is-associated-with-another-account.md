# Why am I seeing a message that my subscription is associated with another account?

Updated: 3 days ago

## Overview

If you’re trying to subscribe to ChatGPT on iOS or Android and see a message saying your subscription or purchase is associated with another ChatGPT account, it usually means your App Store or Play Store account (Apple ID or Google Play account) is already linked to a different ChatGPT account.  
  
This can be confusing—especially if you’re signing in with what feels like the “right” account—so below we explain what’s happening and how to resolve it.

## Common error messages you may see

You might see a message like:  
  
“This subscription is associated with another OpenAI account. Please login with that account to access it, or visit help.openai.com for support.”  
  
These messages indicate that your app store account is already linked to a different ChatGPT account.

## What to do if you see this message

#### Sign in to the original ChatGPT account

If you already have an active subscription:

1. Sign out of ChatGPT
2. Sign back in using the **same ChatGPT account** that originally made the purchase

Your subscription should appear once you’re signed in to the correct account.

#### Avoid re‑purchasing on a different account

Trying to purchase again while signed into another ChatGPT account can lead to confusion or duplicate charges. If you want to use ChatGPT under a different account, you’ll need to manage the subscription separately, from your Google play or Apple App store account.  
  
Also review: [How do I avoid being charged twice if I subscribe to ChatGPT on iOS, Android, and the web?](/en/articles/20001043-how-do-i-avoid-being-charged-twice-if-i-subscribe-to-chatgpt-on-ios-android-and-the-web)

#### Request a refund if you were charged unexpectedly

If you believe you were charged incorrectly:

* **On iOS:** Request a refund directly through Apple
* **On Android:** Request a refund through Google Play, or contact us.

## Frequently asked questions

**How do mobile subscriptions work?**  
  
When you purchase a ChatGPT subscription on iOS or Android:

* The subscription is tied to **both** your **app store account** (Apple ID or Google Play account), and the **ChatGPT account** you were signed into at the time of purchase
* Once linked, that app store account can’t be used to purchase subscriptions for a *different* ChatGPT account

Because of this, if you later sign in to ChatGPT with another account and try to subscribe again, you may see an error—even though you’re using the same phone or app store account.  
  
  
 **Can I transfer my subscription to another ChatGPT account?**  
  
No. Mobile subscriptions can’t be transferred between ChatGPT accounts.  
  
  
 **Can I share one mobile subscription across multiple ChatGPT accounts?**  
  
No. Each mobile subscription is tied to a single ChatGPT account.  
  
  
 **What if I created a new ChatGPT account after subscribing?**  
  
You’ll need to sign back into the **original ChatGPT account** that made the purchase to access your subscription.  
  
  
 **Will uninstalling or reinstalling the app fix this?**  
  
No. Reinstalling the app won’t change which ChatGPT account your subscription is linked to.  
  
  
 **I see an error but the charge still went through—what should I do?**  
  
Sign in to the original ChatGPT account first. If the subscription doesn’t appear there, request a refund through for the applicable platfrom where you attempted the subscription: [How do I request a refund for my ChatGPT subscription?](https://help.openai.com/articles/7232895-how-do-i-request-a-refund-for-my-chatgpt-subscription)  
  
 **Where can I get more help?**  
  
If you’re still having trouble accessing your subscription, contact us for additional support.
