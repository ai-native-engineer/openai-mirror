# ChatGPT Shared Links FAQ

Generate a unique URL to be able share a link to one of your ChatGPT conversations

Updated: 2 days ago

**Note**: Data retention for certain services may be affected by recent legal developments – please see our [blog post](https://openai.com/index/response-to-nyt-data-demands/) for more details.

## What are shared links?

Shared links are a feature that allow users to generate a unique URL for a ChatGPT conversation, which can then be shared with friends, colleagues, and collaborators. Shared links offer a new way for users to share their ChatGPT conversations, replacing the old and burdensome method of sharing screenshots.

## Where can I find shared links?

Shared links are currently available on our website, [chatgpt.com](https://chatgpt.com/chat), and to all users in the iOS and Android apps.

## How do I create a shared link?

You can share a chat from the sidebar, or by using the share button on the top right of the chat screen.  
  
On desktop web, you'll be able to preview the conversation snapshot you're about to send and there are options to share the link directly to popular social networks. On mobile web, ChatGPT may open your device's native share sheet instead, so available share options can differ by device and browser.  
  
When you're ready you can click the button to copy the link to share it.

## How do I update a shared link after it is created?

You can open up the link share for that conversation the same way as you shared the conversation originally.

## Are shared links public? Who can access my shared links?

Anyone who has access to a shared link can view the linked conversation. We encourage you not to share any sensitive content, as anyone with the link can access the conversation or share the link with other people.  
  
If you've created a link that you no longer want to be public, you can delete the link or clear the conversation. The conversation will no longer be accessible via the shared link, but if a user imported the conversation into their chat history, deleting your link will not remove the conversation from their chat history.

## What information about me is included in the shared link I generate?

Shared links will not include your name or any other personal information. Please keep in mind that anyone with the link can view the conversation in a shared link.

## How much of my conversation is included in a shared link?

Shared links can include different amounts of content depending on where you create the link. If you share a chat from the sidebar or from the share button at the top of the chat, the link includes a snapshot of the conversation up to the point it is shared. If a response is still generating when you share, the link may use the latest completed visible message instead, so the in-progress turn may not appear unless you create or update the shared link again after it finishes. If you use the share option on an individual assistant response, the link is scoped to that response rather than the full conversation.

For example, if I have a conversation with 10 prompts with responses then create a shared link to give out then when the recipient opens up that shared link they will not only see the most recent prompt / response but the entire history of prompts / responses up to that point which the shared link was created.

## What are the use cases of shared links?

**A. Sharing a specific conversation or message**  
  
This is particularly useful when you want to share a specific piece of information or a discussion with someone who is not part of the original conversation. This could be a notable example of a conversation, a specific discussion that needs to be referenced, or an important message that needs to be highlighted.  
  
  
**B. Sharing content with external collaborators**  
  
Shared links can be used to share content with people outside of your organization. This is useful when you have collaborators, such as contractors or clients, who need to see specific messages or conversations.  
  
  
**C. Creating a reference point for future discussions**  
  
Shared links can be used to create a reference point for future discussions. This can be particularly useful when you want to refer back to a previous conversation or message in the future.

## If I delete the original ChatGPT conversation does that delete the shared link?

Yes, if you delete the original ChatGPT conversation, the shared link will also be deleted. This means that the content will no longer be accessible via the shared link.  
  
Please keep in mind that shared links allow viewers to import the conversation into their own chat history, so if you delete a shared link, the conversation will no longer be accessible via the shared link, but if a user imported the conversation into their chat history, the content may still be accessible from their chat history.

## If I delete my account are my shared links deleted?

Yes, if you delete your account, all of your shared links will also be deleted. At that point those shared links will no longer be accessible or viewable by anyone.  
  
Please keep in mind that shared links allow viewers to import the conversation into their own chat history, so if you delete a shared link, the conversation will no longer be accessible via the shared link, but if a user imported the conversation into their chat history, the content may still be accessible from their chat history.

## Is there any option to continue the conversation for a shared link?

No, the option to continue a conversation from a shared link has been deprecated for users across all plans in ChatGPT. If the user replies to the conversation shared with them, it will create a copy of the conversation in their conversation history, which won't be deleted if you choose to delete a shared conversation.

## Data Export; Are my shared links included when I export my data from ChatGPT?

Yes, shared conversations are included in ChatGPT data export.

## How do I limit access / permission to the shared link?

Unfortunately we currently do not have granular permissions for shared links. Anyone who has access to a shared link can view the linked conversation. We encourage you not to share any sensitive content, as anyone with the link can access the conversation or share the link with other people.

## How do I set the expiration date for a shared link?

We do not currently offer this functionality. See below for how to delete or disable your shared links.

## How do I know a shared link is safe to click / authentically from OpenAI?

The URL for ChatGPT shared links starts with: `https://chatgpt.com/share/`

Always be cautious before clicking on links and make sure the shared link is coming from a trusted source.  
  
  
**`Never click on a suspicious link.`**

## What's the structure of the URL of a shared link?

```
https://chatgpt.com/share/<conversation-ID>
```

## How do I report harmful or illegal content in a shared link?

You can report a shared link to us by clicking “Report conversation” at the top of the screen. You will be asked to describe the issue with the content and to identify the nature of your report. You do not need to be signed in to submit content reports.

## Where do I access my settings to see my shared links?

In [chatgpt.com](https://chat.openai.com/chat) in the bottom left of the page click on your name to open the menu then click on Settings.  
  
Then in the section that appears click on **Data controls** then click **Shared links** setting.

## What happens to my shared links after I disable Chat History & Training in my ChatGPT Data controls?

Nothing happens to them and they are not deleted.  
  
Similar to when you have existing conversations in your history, disabling history doesn't delete them, it just hides your history and puts you in basically an incognito mode.  
  
Once you disable Chat History & Training the share button is disabled for conversations.

## How do I delete / invalidate a shared link?

You can click the **trashcan icon under three dots menu** on the conversation itself in [chatgpt.com](https://chatgpt.com/chat) to delete a single chat.  
  
You can also view and manage all your shared link from **Settings > Data Controls**. Locate the **Shared links** entry and click **Manage**. You can then delete individual links, or delete all of them.
