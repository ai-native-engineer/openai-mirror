# Customizing Your ChatGPT Personality

Updated: 2 days ago

A personality is the style and tone ChatGPT uses when responding to you. It combines traits, voice, and behavior to shape how answers feel, whether that is friendly and casual, concise and professional, or something else.  
  
Changing your personality does not change what ChatGPT can or cannot do, or the safety rules it follows. It only guides how ChatGPT communicates, so you can choose a style that feels most useful or enjoyable.  
  
Personality also does not control every type of content you ask ChatGPT to create. For example, if you request an email draft, code snippet, social media post, or résumé, ChatGPT will match the tone and style to your instructions and the context, not necessarily to the personality you selected. If you have Friendly selected and ask for Python code to calculate shipping costs, ChatGPT will provide clear, functional code based on your request. It will not add Friendly’s usual reflective or conversational style.  
  
Your selected personality works alongside any saved memories or custom instructions you’ve set. This can add nuance to any personality you choose. If a saved memory contains guidance that conflicts with a personality’s style, such as a preference for serious and professional responses, it may override or reduce the visible traits of that personality. Instructions you give during a conversation can also adjust or obscure a personality’s behavior.

## Choose your ChatGPT personality

### Web

To choose a ChatGPT personality on web:

* Select your profile icon in the bottom-right corner.
* Select **Personalization**.
* Use the dropdown next to **Base style and tone** to select a personality.

When you change your personality, the update applies across all chats, including existing conversations.

### iOS and Android

To choose a ChatGPT personality on iOS or Android:

* Select your profile icon to open **Settings**.
* Go to **Personalization**.
* Use **Base style and tone** to select a personality.

## Overview of ChatGPT personalities

You can use Default, the standard ChatGPT style, or choose from the personalities listed below. Default is clear and neutral. Each other personality has its own style and tone.

### Professional

Polished and precise. Uses formal language and professional writing conventions.

**What to expect**

* Formal tone with polished, precise phrasing.
* Professional writing conventions, including clear structure and organization.
* Comfortable use of business jargon for the specific professional domain you prompt for.
* In languages with different conjugations for formality, uses forms suitable for the workplace.

**Best for**

* Workplace communication and documentation that needs a polished, professional tone.
* Users who want precise, formal language with appropriate business jargon.
* Situations where workplace-appropriate formality and conjugations are important.

### Friendly

Warm and chatty, reflecting your thoughts back with calm clarity and light wit.

**What to expect**

* Responses that surface trade-offs and likely outcomes.
* An occasional clarifying question or two, if it helps provide better guidance.
* Guidance aimed at helping users make their own decisions.

**Best for**

* Users who want a conversational sounding board.
* Decision support, personal reflection, and planning.

### Candid

Direct and encouraging. Gives honest feedback with clear next steps.

**What to expect**

* Straightforward answers that focus on the core issue.
* Clear callouts of risks, gaps, and trade-offs, paired with constructive suggestions.
* Encouragement that balances blunt honesty with support and motivation.
* Less small talk and more actionable guidance.

**Best for**

* Users who want no-nonsense, plain-spoken input.
* Gut checks on plans, drafts, or decisions where sugarcoating would get in the way.
* Moments when you feel stuck and need someone to “just say it” while still rooting for you.

### Quirky

Playful and imaginative. Uses humor and unexpected ideas to explore your questions.

**What to expect**

* A light, playful tone with jokes or offbeat observations.
* Creative metaphors, stories, or thought experiments to explain concepts.
* Real answers, wrapped in imaginative framing instead of dry exposition.
* Occasional surprises that make serious topics feel lighter and more fun.

**Best for**

* Users who enjoy a more playful, less formal style.
* Brainstorming, creative writing, and “what if?” explorations.
* Moments when you want problem-solving or learning to feel fun, not stiff.

### Efficient

Concise and plain. Delivers direct answers without extra words.

**What to expect**

* An immediate direct answer first, followed by concise reasoning or steps.
* Clear mapping of problems into inputs, levers, and outputs, when applicable.
* Occasional citations or references for factual claims.

**Best for**

* Users who want direct, fast, unambiguous instructions.
* Technical tasks, code walkthroughs, checklists, and troubleshooting.

### Cynical

Sarcastic and dry. Delivers blunt help with wit. Often teases, but provides direct, practical answers when it matters.

**What to expect**

* Candid responses that may include a sarcastic observation.
* An irreverent tone that is not hostile and signals patience limits clearly.

**Best for**

* Users who want sarcastic, entertaining replies that are still actionable.
* Concrete guidance alongside the snark.
* Creative brainstorming where an edged voice is fine.
