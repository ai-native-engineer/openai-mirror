# Voice Dictation - FAQ

Learn what happens when you use dictation (the microphone icon) in ChatGPT to record an audio message.

Updated: 3 days ago

**Note**: Data retention for certain services may be affected by recent legal developments – please see our [**blog post**](https://openai.com/index/response-to-nyt-data-demands/) for more details

## How does voice dictation work?

When you press the microphone icon to record an audio message, the recorded audio is sent to our models to be transcribed. The transcription is returned as text, which you can edit before sending as a user message.

## How long do you retain audio from dictation?

Audio from dictation will be retained for as long as the chat is part of your chat history. When you delete the chat, we’ll also delete the associated audio clip within 30 days unless we need to keep it for security or legal reasons or if you previously shared your audio with us to train our models and the audio clip was previously disassociated from your account.

## Do you train on audio from voice dictation?

If you are a consumer user and have chosen to share audio to improve our models, we may train on audio from dictation.  
  
By default, we do not train on any inputs or outputs from our products for business users. Please see our [Enterprise Privacy page](https://openai.com/enterprise-privacy) for information on how we handle business data.

## How can I stop sharing audio from dictation?

You can stop sharing through the data controls page in your ChatGPT settings. Just disable the “Include your audio recordings” toggle, or turn off “Improve the model for everyone” entirely. Note that this will stop sharing all audio across dictation and voice chat.
