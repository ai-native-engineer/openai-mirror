I use Codex to not just try one idea but
try like a hundred ideas.
I'm Ankur Goyal, founder and CEO of
Braintrust, and this is what Codex

[music] is being able to try out
customer feature requests in real-time.
Our customers are very, very active with
their feedback about Braintrust, and one
of the things we encourage is that they
give us feature requests. So, previously
if someone gave us a feature request, it
would enter into a backlog and then get
prioritized. Now, what I'll often do is
just copy-paste the Slack message into
Codex and then create a preview branch
and show that feature request to the
customer within like 10 minutes. And the
really cool thing about that is we get
to iterate and ideate on the feature
request with the [music] customer in
real-time. With this workflow, not only
does the user get immediate feedback,
but you actually get them playing with
the feature request while it's in this
kind of molten phase. And so, you get
really, really high-quality, interesting
feedback.
