# AI Communication Toolkit

This toolkit contains a series of email templates designed to support various stages of your ChatGPT Edu deployment.

## How to use these templates

These templates are intended as starting points to help you communicate your ChatGPT Edu launch with your campus community. We encourage you to edit and customize the content to reflect your university’s unique needs, audience, and internal structure.

## Announcing ChatGPT Edu to Students

**Purpose**: Introduction to ChatGPT and overview of self-serve resources

**Timing**: After SSO has been completed and user accounts have been provisioned

**Email template**:

`We’re thrilled to announce that ChatGPT Edu is now available at [University Name]! AI is evolving rapidly, and this is your opportunity to discover how ChatGPT can help streamline coursework, research projects, and creative endeavors. From analyzing complex data to generating fresh ideas, ChatGPT has the potential to elevate how you learn and collaborate.`

`The launch of ChatGPT Edu marks an exciting step in the intersection of education and AI, providing [University Name] students with a powerful tool to enhance learning, spark creativity, and support research. ## Use Case Inspiration for Students

**Purpose:** Inspire students with common use cases of ChatGPT for students, and remind them of available resources

**Timing:** ~2 weeks after accounts are provisioned

**Email template**:

`Hi [Team/Students],`

`We hope you are taking advantage of all of the resources we shared and finding ways that ChatGPT can help support your work. If you’re looking for some inspiration, we’re excited to share a list of use cases and sample prompts for [University Name] students:`

`Adaptive Study Plan Generator`

`Use Case:` `Generate a personalized and dynamic study schedule based on your performance, upcoming deadlines, and learning style.`

`Example Prompt:` `“Create a personalized study plan for my upcoming [subject] exams, incorporating these specific areas where I need extra practice [list them]. Include time allocations, recommended practice problems, and review sessions, adjusting difficulty based on my progress.”`

`Real-Time Peer Review & Feedback`

`Use Case:` `Get structured, constructive commentary on essays, presentations, or creative projects.`

`Example Prompt:` `“Review my [essay/outline/presentation] on [topic]. Provide feedback organized into strengths, weaknesses, and recommended changes, along with at least three additional resource suggestions to improve my arguments.”`

`Custom Research Assistant`

`Use Case:` `Streamline research by summarizing articles, identifying gaps in literature, and suggesting relevant resources.`

`Example Prompt:` `“Help me research [topic]. Summarize the attached sources [links] and group them by recurring themes. Identify any research gaps, and suggest additional resources that deepen my understanding. Provide a bulleted summary for each theme.”`

`Practice Real-World Scenarios`

`Use Case:` `Simulate hands-on scenarios—like business negotiations, lab experiments, or policy debates—to build practical skills.`

`Example Prompt:` `“Simulate a real-world negotiation between a sports management firm and a sponsor. Present potential objections and pushbacks. Offer feedback on my negotiation style and propose improvements for communication, strategy, and relationship management.”`

`Personalized Career Pathway Exploration`

`Use Case:` `Discover potential career paths that align with personal interests, academic strengths, and market trends.`

`Example Prompt:` `“Based on my major in [subject] and my interests in [fields], suggest at least three relevant career paths. Include typical responsibilities, in-demand skills, internship ideas, and courses or certifications that would boost my employability.”`

`Collaborative Project Management`

`Use Case:` `Divide group project tasks, set realistic timelines, and establish communication protocols to ensure timely completion.`

`Example Prompt:` `“Plan our group project on [topic] by breaking it into major milestones, assigning tasks to each member based on their strengths, and setting deadlines. Include a schedule for updates and checkpoints to ensure we stay on track.”`

`Use Case:` `Draft or revise institutional policies (e.g., onboarding, compliance) to ensure alignment with regulations and best practices.`

`Example Prompt:``“Draft a comprehensive onboarding checklist for new faculty members, ensuring alignment with institutional policies and best practices. `Use Case:` `Plan and coordinate campus-wide events or departmental conferences, including scheduling and communication strategies.`

`Use Case:` `Analyze and improve financial aid distribution, ensuring equity and maximum student support.`

`Example Prompt:``“Analyze our financial aid distribution data from [specific time period] to identify gaps and opportunities for greater impact. Suggest data-driven methods to reallocate resources, focusing on underrepresented groups or unmet financial needs. Propose a monitoring plan for ongoing adjustments.”`

### `Crisis Response Planning:`

`Use Case:` `Develop emergency preparedness plans for scenarios like natural disasters, health crises, or campus-wide disruptions.`

`Example Prompt:``“Develop a crisis response plan for a potential [type of disaster] impacting our campus. Outline immediate actions, communication channels, evacuation protocols, and long-term recovery strategies. Tailor the plan to local safety regulations and resources.”`

### `Alumni Engagement Strategy`

`Use Case:` `Cultivate stronger alumni relationships through personalized outreach and event planning.`

`Example Prompt:` `“Create an alumni engagement strategy for [institution name], using data on past alumni interactions. Segment communications based on interests and involvement levels. Include targeted outreach, both digital and in-person events, and steps for maintaining alumni support long-term.”`
