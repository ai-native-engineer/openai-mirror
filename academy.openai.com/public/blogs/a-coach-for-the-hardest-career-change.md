## How AI Ready Veteran uses ChatGPT to personalize military-to-civilian transition support for thousands

![A coach for the hardest career change](https://cdn.gradual.com/images/https://d2xo500swnpgl1.cloudfront.net/uploads/oaiacademy/AI-Ready-Vet-ce00c9cc-ac68-4c20-a7aa-0a647b971e68-1766068720397.jpeg?fit=scale-down&width=1200)

With ChatGPT, Navy veteran and college educator [Stephen K. Hudson, (USN, Ret)](https://www.linkedin.com/in/stephen-k-hudson-usn-ret-00b658111/) is rebuilding the military‑to‑civilian transition and delivering personalized, just‑in‑time career coaching at scale to address a widespread need among military members, veterans, and their families.
Hudson served 20 years in the Navy as a data analyst before moving into private‑sector leadership and, later, the classroom. He founded [AI Ready Veteran, Inc](https://www.linkedin.com/company/aireadyveteran/), a nonprofit, to turn those lessons into action, and launched its first AI-driven tools in May 2024. AI Ready Veteran already serves more than 1,000 users.
Veterans face recurring hurdles on day one of transition: translating military skills to the right civilian roles, navigating multi‑round interviews, dealing with culture shock in the civilian workplace, and finding work that feels meaningful. Research underscores the stakes, since nearly half of veterans leave their first civilian job within a year.
Hudson’s answer is a set of AI-driven tools (custom GPTs) for each step of a veteran’s job search and onboarding. A translator maps Military Occupational Specialties and terminology to civilian competencies, and then surfaces target roles at specific employers. A resume builder and interview coach creates tailored applications, simulates interview sequences from HR to VP-level, and enables live practice in ChatGPT’s voice mode. A “military transition coach” helps veterans decode norms around feedback, escalation, and collaboration once they’ve been hired. Hudson says that adapting to that is “a culture shock” for anyone used to a tight chain of command. “It’s hyper‑personalized for the veteran transition, so the guidance feels specific to you,” he said at Semafor’s World Economic Summit last month in Washington, DC, where AI Ready Veteran was showcased as an OpenAI partner.
At another event, this one a workshop for veterans in transition, a surface warfare officer was preparing to leave active duty, and was unsure about next steps. She watched Hudson’s platform list her civilian skill set, name companies “looking for people just like her,” and even outline a credible path to launch a consulting business which the “entrepreneurial coach” could be used to help her launch.
Interview prep produces frequent wins. One veteran told Hudson that using his tools had taught him more about the company he wanted to work for than the person interviewing him even knew. Hudson is clear‑eyed about the division of labor: “I can’t get the job for them, but our tools prepare them for the interview and give them the confidence to nail it."
ChatGPT gives Hudson a way to scale mentorship. Military members, veterans, and their families get structured guidance; employers meet mission‑ready teammates who can translate experience into impact; and ChatGPT is the coach that makes the transition faster, clearer, and more personal.
